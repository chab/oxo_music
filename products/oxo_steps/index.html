Oxo Steps

MIDI step sequencer / arpeggiator

Change chord, everything follows

The sequencer we couldn't find: enter one chord, derive six independent sequences from it.

Example

  • Track 1 (Channel 1): Bass line from the chord's lowest note.
  • Track 2 (Channel 2): Chord stabs from selected chord notes, or fixed notes if preferred.
  • Track 3 (Channel 3): Arpeggio using all notes, polymetric + 1 octave every 7 steps
  • Track 4 (Channel 2): Random in-key notes every now and then (Note the channel : it is the same instrument playing the chord).

Why ?

Oxo Steps was designed for iPad first: gestures, touch flow, and the all-in-one portable setup make it the ideal platform. With enough interest, we may build the VST/AU version. Feel free to contact us.

Feature highlights

  • Monophonic or polyphonic tracks
  • Polymetric lanes (e.g. gate over 16 steps, notes over 12, octave over 6)
  • Pattern direction: forward, backward, ping-pong, random
  • Predictable probability lane controls (e.g. last bar of 4)
  • Two CC lanes to sequence your favourite plugin knobs (CC = Continuous Controller MIDI messages)
  • Chord memory
  • Track offset in ms: sync external hardware synths to the millisecond
  • Hold or legato (holds across patterns)
  • Chord progression presets (save and recall your favourite progressions, then try them on the current sequences)
  • Export as MIDI file: record your performance, continue in your DAW
  • Performance window: everything you need to perform in one place. Switch scenes, a knob for every CC
